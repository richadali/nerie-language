What is an IVCS?

The Integrated Village Co-operative Societies (IVCS) are unique home-grown village/community based organizations/ credit cooperative societies initiated by the Cooperation Department, Government of Meghalaya in 2011-12 which are grown and nurtured under the Meghalaya Livelihood and Access to Markets Project (Megha-LAMP).

Important points to be noted on formation of IVCS

  • An IVCS should ideally be a single village IVCS provided the village has at least 150 to 200 Households.
  • Each IVCS should strive to cover all Households and have 300 or more Members.
  • The IVCS are formed and develop in collaboration and close coordination with the Office of the Registrar of Cooperative, Societies, Government of Meghalaya.
  • It may be shared that performing IVCS have managed to obtain various kinds of support, in terms of materials, funds, infrastructure from Government and Government Agencies, exposure and learning and active linkage with MCAB and other banks.

Who can be a member in an IVCS?

  • Membership in an IVCS is opened to individuals above the age of 18 years and lives in the village(s) covered by the IVCS.
  • Membership in an IVCS will also be open to SHGs/ Members of SHGs, Producer Groups/Members of PGs which operate/ who lives in the village(s) covered by IVCS.
  • Minimum of 15 adults can start, as promoters, the process of forming an IVCS. Of the 15 promoters, efforts should be made that at least 05 will be women.
  • While mobilizing membership, minimum 50% of them should be women.

What benefits will members get by forming an IVCS?

The IVCS would be a primary cooperative society with multiple services to:

Finance

Provide access to financial services especially savings and loans (credit) to its members.

Farming

Custom Hiring Centres (CHCs) are also set up in the IVCS where farm machineries and equipments are provided to the IVCS

Marketing

Collective Marketing Centres (CMCs) for storing crops, vegetables and other products.

Other Services

Act as a service provider for different entities which would like to reach the village households through the medium of the IVCS.

Key message for the Members

  • IVCS is an institution promoted by the people and is not a government institution.
  • The IVCS is an institution promoted by the people and is not a government institution.
  • The participation of all residents in the village(s) covered by the IVCS is most important as it is this which will make the IVCS strong.
  • It should be the endeavour of the promoters and members of the Managing Committees of the IVCS to make all adults of all households in the village(s) to become members of the IVCS.
