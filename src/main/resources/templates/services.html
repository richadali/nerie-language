What benefits will members get by forming an IVCS?

The IVCS would be a primary cooperative society with multiple services to:

Provide access to financial services especially savings and loans (credit) to its members. Producer Groups are formed out of members cultivating same product or undertaking same economic activity are provided with interest free returnable funds for increasing their activities. Many IVCS have received interest free loans under the State Government’s Piggery Mission for pig rearing and breeding activities.

Sell to its members and other customers, inputs and other essential requirements that have to be sourced from outside the village. Custom Hiring Centres (CHCs) are also set up in the IVCS where farm machineries and equipments are provided to the IVCS which can hire them to their members on payment of hiring charges.

Engage in aggregation of crops/ products of members with a view to marketing the same advantageously to realize better prices. The IVCS or its members can also engage in processing activities as well. Here the project would establish Collective Marketing Centres (CMCs) for storing crops, vegetables and other products. Cold Storages are also set up which operate on solar energy. IVCS are also assisted in setting up Food processing units.

Act as a service provider for different entities which would like to reach the village households through the medium of the IVCS for variety of objectives such as marketing of products – both financial and non-financial.

Under the project, a number of capacity building programmes through trainings, workshops, exposure visits (within and outside the State) are conducted free of cost from time to time for the office bearers and members of the IVCS. The programmes help office bearers develop skills in various fields like, financial literacy and maintenance of books of accounts, registers etc., mobilization, collective marketing and managing business establishments
